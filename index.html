Frequently Asked Questions

How do I use the ND Filter Calculator?

1) Enter your base shutter speed (the speed without filters), 2) Tap "Add Filter" and select from common ND filters (ND8, ND64, ND1000, etc.) or enter custom stop values, 3) Stack multiple filters by adding more, 4) View the calculated new shutter speed instantly, 5) Optionally tap "Start Timer" to begin a countdown with that duration.

How do I save a preset?

In any calculator tool (ND Filter, Timer, Reciprocity), configure your settings, then tap the "Save as Preset" button. Give it a descriptive name like "ND64+ND8 Landscape" or "Portra 400 5min". Free users can save up to 5 presets; Pro users have unlimited presets.

How do I use saved presets?

Tap the "Presets" section in the Home screen or use the preset button (bookmark icon) in individual tools. Your saved presets appear in a list - tap any preset to instantly load those settings into the calculator.

What is Night Mode and how do I enable it?

Night Mode is a special ultra-dark interface with red/low-blue colors designed to preserve your night vision during astrophotography and night shooting. Toggle Night Mode on/off using the moon icon in the Home screen header. The setting persists across app launches.

How do I use the Bulb Timer?

1) Enter your desired exposure time or load a preset, 2) Tap "Start" to begin the countdown, 3) The timer shows large, easy-to-read digits, 4) Toggle "Keep Screen Awake" to prevent your device from sleeping, 5) When time expires, you'll get a vibration and/or sound alert (configurable in settings).

How does the Exposure Converter work?

The Exposure Converter maintains the same exposure value (EV) while changing settings. Set your current aperture, shutter speed, and ISO. Then adjust any value - the others automatically recalculate to maintain equivalent exposure. Perfect for understanding exposure relationships or adapting to different shooting constraints.

Which film stocks are supported in the Reciprocity Calculator?

The free version includes popular film stocks: Kodak Portra 160/400/800, Kodak Ektar 100, Ilford HP5 Plus, Ilford Delta 100/400/3200, Ilford FP4 Plus, Fuji Acros 100, and more. Pro users get access to an extended database with additional film stocks and custom curve adjustments.

How do I use the Film Reciprocity Calculator?

1) Select your film stock from the database, 2) Enter your meter-indicated exposure time, 3) View the corrected exposure time accounting for reciprocity failure, 4) Optionally tap "Start Timer" to time the corrected exposure, 5) Save frequently-used film/time combinations as presets.

What are the 250 Rule, 300 Rule, and NPF Rule?

These are methods to calculate maximum shutter speed for astrophotography before stars start trailing:

  • 250 Rule: 250 √∑ focal length = max seconds. Optimized for modern high-resolution cameras (24MP+)
  • 300 Rule: 300 √∑ focal length = max seconds. Traditional rule, more conservative
  • NPF Rule: Advanced formula considering pixel pitch, aperture, and declination for pinpoint star accuracy with adjustable K-factor (default: K=20 conservative)
How do I use the Astrophotography Calculator?

1) Select your rule type (250, 300, or NPF), 2) Enter your lens focal length, 3) For NPF: also enter aperture, camera megapixels, and optionally adjust K-factor and declination, 4) View the calculated maximum shutter speed, 5) Tap "Start Timer" to time your exposure, 6) Save your camera/lens combinations as presets for quick access.
